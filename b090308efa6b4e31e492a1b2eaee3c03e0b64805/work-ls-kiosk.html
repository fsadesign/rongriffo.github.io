Life Storage Kiosk

A self-service kiosk for customers to access their storage units and pay their account balance.

Summary

The Life Storage self-service kiosk provides customers with a seamless and convenient way to manage their storage needs. This innovative solution allows users to rent units, make payments, and access their storage accounts without the need for in-person assistance, enhancing customer convenience and operational efficiency.

Key Features:
  • User-Friendly Interface: Intuitive touch-screen navigation designed for all age groups.
  • Real-Time Availability: Customers can view available units, prices, and promotions instantly.
  • Secure Payment Processing: Multiple payment options, including credit/debit cards and mobile payments, ensuring a secure transaction experience.
  • 24/7 Accessibility: Customers can access the kiosk any time of day for ultimate flexibility.

Process

1. Discovery & Research
  • User Interviews: Conducted interviews with 20 potential users to identify pain points in the current rental process.
  • Competitor Analysis: Researched 5 competitor self-service systems to understand industry standards and potential improvements.
  • Surveys: Deployed a survey to 200 existing customers to gather insights about their preferences for in-person vs. self-service.
2. Personas & User Flows

Created 3 primary user personas:

  • First-Time Renter: Needs guidance on the process.
  • Returning Customer: Looks for speed and efficiency.
  • Business Customer: Requires recurring management and payments.

Mapped out key user flows for each persona, focusing on actions such as:

  • Renting a new unit
  • Making a payment
  • Managing account settings
3. Wireframing & Prototyping
  • Developed low-fidelity wireframes to visualize key screens such as:
    • Welcome Screen
    • Unit Selection
    • Payment Processing
  • Tested wireframes in 3 rounds with 10 users per round, iterating based on feedback.
  • Built an interactive high-fidelity prototype for usability testing.
4. Usability Testing
  • Task completion time: Users completed transactions 35% faster compared to the old in-person method.
  • Error rate: Reduced by 25% compared to manual processes.
  • User satisfaction: 90% of participants rated the kiosk experience as "satisfying" or "very satisfying."
5. UI Design & Development Handoff
  • Designed a clean, intuitive interface with large buttons and clear navigation.
  • Color coding was implemented for various actions, e.g., red for cancellations, green for successful transactions.
  • After final iterations, the design was handed off to developers with detailed annotations and design assets.

UX Results

After the launch of the self-service kiosks at 50 Life Storage locations, a post-launch analysis was conducted to measure the impact.

  • User Adoption: 65% of customers preferred the kiosk over in-person service within 3 months of launch.
  • Transaction Time: Average transaction time decreased from 15 minutes (in-person) to 5 minutes (self-service).
  • Error Rate: Errors related to unit rental were reduced by 40% post-launch.
  • Customer Satisfaction: A follow-up survey with 500 users showed an 85% satisfaction rate with the self-service experience.
  • Return on Investment (ROI): Life Storage saved an estimated $250,000 in staffing costs in the first quarter after implementation, with an expected annual savings of $1 million.

The Life Storage self-service kiosk UX process was highly effective, significantly improving the customer experience by making transactions faster, more intuitive, and error-free. The successful rollout demonstrated the value of user-centered design and resulted in high adoption rates and substantial cost savings.
