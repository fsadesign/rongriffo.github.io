Life Storage Redesign

The first fully responsive website in the self storage industry.

Summary

I was responsible for leading the UX redesign of LifeStorage.com, a self-storage service provider. The goal was to optimize the user experience, focusing on improving the ease of renting storage units online, enhancing mobile responsiveness, and boosting overall conversions. My approach involved thorough user research, iterative design, and testing, all aimed at simplifying the customer journey and reducing friction points in the rental process.

Process

1. Discovery & Research
  • Stakeholder Interviews: Collaborated with LifeStorage’s business stakeholders to clearly define goals—namely, increasing storage unit rentals and improving mobile engagement.
  • User Research: Conducted interviews with a diverse group of 20 users to uncover pain points. I found that users often struggled with unclear pricing and unit availability, especially on mobile devices.
  • Competitive Analysis: Evaluated UX patterns of major competitors and identified key areas where LifeStorage.com could differentiate itself through a more streamlined interface.
  • Analytics Review: Analyzed site data and found that the bounce rate on the unit selection page was 33%, and the checkout abandonment rate was as high as 47%.
2. Personas & Journey Mapping
  • Developed 3 core user personas:
    • College Student: Short-term storage needs
    • Small Business Owner: Long-term, larger storage
    • Relocating Family: Mid-term, residential storage
  • Created detailed journey maps for these personas, highlighting moments of frustration and opportunities for improvement in the search, selection, and checkout stages.
3. Wireframes & Prototyping
  • Designed low-fidelity wireframes that focused on simplifying the search and booking flow, particularly for mobile users.
  • Created interactive prototypes to test with users, ensuring the design addressed critical pain points like complicated pricing and unclear next steps during checkout.
4. Usability Testing
  • Conducted usability tests with 12 users, collecting feedback on the new designs.
  • Key insights included:
    • Users wanted a clearer way to compare unit sizes and prices.
    • Reducing the number of steps in the checkout process made users feel more confident in completing their bookings.
    • A progress indicator significantly reduced drop-off rates.
5. Design Iteration & Handoff
  • Based on usability testing results, I refined the designs to improve the unit comparison feature and simplified the user interface for better mobile usability.
  • Worked closely with the development team to ensure the final product maintained design integrity while being optimized for performance.

Result

  • Increase in Conversion Rates:
    • Before: 6% of visitors completed a rental
    • After: 9% conversion rate (50% increase)
  • Reduction in Bounce Rates:
    • Before: 33% bounce rate on storage unit selection pages
    • After: 22% bounce rate (33% improvement)
  • Checkout Abandonment Rate:
    • Before: 47% checkout abandonment
    • After: 28% abandonment rate (40% reduction)
  • Improved Mobile Engagement:
    • Before: 30% of bookings completed via mobile
    • After: 45% of bookings (50% increase)
  • User Satisfaction:
    • Before: 6.4/10 average satisfaction score
    • After: 8.5/10 average satisfaction score

The new design resulted in a more streamlined, user-friendly experience that addressed user pain points, leading to increased conversions, reduced bounce rates, and higher user satisfaction.
